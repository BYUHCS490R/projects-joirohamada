Flavors of Mexico

Mexican food is worldwide known for being one of the most delicious and enjoyable to eat. The country's history and culture are deeply interconnected with the gastronomy, combining influences from Mesoamerica and Europe.

Let's not forget to mention that corn is the foundation of many dishes. And spicy food is one of the specialties of Mexican cuisine; tortillas and corn made food are a daily base for millions of Mexicans.

Next, I would like to present to you a list of the most famous dishes in Mexico.


Tacos

This dish consists of a tortilla(folded or rolled) and it contains some type of filling; typically, this is a meat (beef is commonly added), along with vegetables such as raw onion, cilantro, and lime, as well as hot sauces (green and red) and guacamole.

Chilaquiles

Chilaquiles are the essential iconic Mexican breakfast, and they are usually prepared at home.

There are two types of chilaquiles: green chilaquiles and red chilaquiles. They are prepared using “totopos” (tortilla chips), which are then bathed in sauce. To add even more flavor, we also top them with chicken, cream, cheese, rice, and fried eggs.

Image source: Source


Tamales

Tamales are one of the oldest and most famous dishes in Mexico. The word "tamal" comes from the indigenous Nahuatl language, meaning "wrapped."

To prepare tamales, we need to use corn masa and spread it onto a dried banana leaf. Inside, some type of meat with sauce is placed, and they are then steamed. Tamales are usually accompanied by a drink similar to hot chocolate called atole.

Image source: Source


Enchiladas

Enchiladas consist of corn tortillas cooked in oil, bathed in a type of sauce, and filled with chicken, meat, or cheese. They are accompanied by lettuce, sour cream, cheese, and raw onion.

There are various ways to present them: green, red, or Swiss enchiladas, enmoladas, enchiladas potosinas, and so on.
